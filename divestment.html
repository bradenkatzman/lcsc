FossilFreeJTS

List College Student Council aims to divest The Jewish Theological Seminary's endowment from companies engaged in the extraction and burning of fossil fuels. We represent current students, alumni and the widespread Jewish community in our campaign to divest from unethical enterprises. We join the worldwide movement for climate justice and believe as Jews we have a moral obligation to ensure a future free of climate chaos.

What is Climate Change?

A change in global or regional climate patterns, in particular a change apparent from the mid to late 20th century onwards and attributed largely to the increased levels of atmospheric carbon dioxide produced by the use of fossil fuels.

According to NASA, at least 97% of actively-publishing climate scientists agree that climate change is “very likely due to human activities.” (climate.nasa.gov)


What's At Stake?

Today, the amount of carbon dioxide is higher than at any time in the last 800,000 years. To avoid the most catastrophic effects of climate change we can only burn less than 500 gigatons of carbon, while the fossil fuel industry currently holds 2,860 gigatons in its reserves

What is Divestment?

The opposite of an investment – it simply means getting rid of stocks, bonds, or investment funds that are unethical or morally ambiguous. By doing so we stigmatize the fossil fuel industry and expose its destructive practices.


What is Divestment at JTS?

Like any other academic institution, JTS has a portfolio of investments so that its endowment makes money. We are demanding that JTS divest ("un-invest") from holdings in fossil fuel companies.


Why Divest?

In order to preserve and protect the earth, something must be done. We must do is divest from companies that engage in the extractions and selling of fossil fuels starting with the Carbon Underground 200, the top 100 public coal and 100 public oil and gas companies.
