Explore NEW YORK CITY!

Whether you're a New York City native or a newcomer to the city that never sleeps, we guarantee that there are things to do on this list which you never knew existed!

LCSC has taken the time to compile a bunch of awesome places in the city that we know you'll have a blast at, all including links to websites, reviews, and pricing information!


Do you know of a place that should be on this list? Please email the info to brkatzman@jtsa.edu!

You can play Beach Volley Ball for free at Pier 25 on Saturdays from 11am-3pm. They request a $5 donation. You can register for a court here
CENTRAL PARK ROWBOATS
YELP REVIEWS
You can rent rowboats (up to 4 people per boat) or non-foot Ocean Kayaks at the Central Park Loeb Boathouse (The Lake off East Drive at 74th Street). Website
Read More
Boat rentals are available daily from 10am-6:30pm. The last boat rental is at 5:30pm and boats much be returned by 6:30pm. The rentals cost $12 for the first hour and $2.50 for each additional 15 minutes. There is a $20 cash deposit required per boat. For more information, check out the Loeb Boathouse website
THE HIGH LINE
YELP REVIEWS
The High Line is a public park built on an historic freight rail line above the streets on Manhattan’s West Side. From Gansevoort St. to West 34th St, between 10th & 11th Ave. Open daily from 7am-10pm. Website
Read More
The park offers a wide array of artwork including site-specific commissions, exhibitions, performances, video programs, and a series of billboard interventions.
Free tours are offered on Tuesdays at 6:30pm (see "High Line Walking Tour" under "Tours").
Also on Tuesday evenings, you can participate in free stargazing, which is offered every Tuesday at sunset (enter at 14th Street and 10th Avenue). This is put on by the Amateur Astronomers Association of New York

BROOKLYN BRIDGE
YELP REVIEWS
A fun and free thing to do in the city is to walk the Brooklyn Bridge! It's a 1.3 mile walk. The views are much prettier when you walk from Brooklyn to Manhattan.
More Info and Directions
Before and/or after your walk, there are tons of restaurants in the area (famous ones include Brooklyn Ice Cream, pizza at Grimaldi's and chocolate at Jacques Torres and there's the Brooklyn Bridge Park. On Sundays, the Smorgasburg Food Market takes place in the area from 11am-6pm.


From Brooklyn: The Brooklyn Bridge Pedestrian Walkway begins at the intersection of Tillary Street and Boerum Place or via an underpass on Washington Street. The underpass is about two blocks from Front Street in DUMBO. This underpass leads up a stairway to a ramp up that brings you onto the Brooklyn Bridge Pedestrian Walkway.

From Manhattan: The Brooklyn Bridge Pedestrian Walk can be accessed from City Hall on the Manhattan side of the East River.
AMATEUR NIGHT AT THE APOLLO
YELP REVIEWS
Amateur Night at the Apollo, a talent competition, has been around for over 75 years, serving as the model for Star Search and American Idol. Website
More Info
Competitions are held nearly every Wednesday evening throughout the year, culminating with the "Super Top Dog" competition. "The executioner" chases unsuccessful contestants from the stage with a hook once audiences begin to boo. Tickets cost either $20, $26, or $32 and can be purchased at the Apollo Theater Box Office (Wednesdays 10am-6pm and Saturdays noon-5pm), by phone call Ticketmaster (800) 745-3000, or online.

For groups of 10 or more, tickets cost $18, $23, or $28. There's also an "A-LIST advance discount" of $5 until midnight the day before performance for those who subscribe to the Apollo Theater email list. Additionally, students can purchase tickets for $12  on the day-of-performance only from 10 a.m. at the Box Office (subject to availability). One ticket per I.D.
